Alberta Anglers Guide - General Information

Alberta offers world-class fishing opportunities with over 600 lakes and rivers. The province is home to diverse fish species including rainbow trout, brown trout, lake trout, northern pike, walleye, and arctic grayling.

FISHING SEASONS
- Most lakes and rivers are open year-round, but specific regulations apply
- Ice fishing is popular from December through March
- Peak summer fishing: June through September
- Spring runoff typically occurs in May

POPULAR FISHING DESTINATIONS
- Bow River (Calgary): Rainbow and brown trout, open year-round
- Lac Ste. Anne: Walleye, northern pike, yellow perch
- Lesser Slave Lake: Walleye, northern pike, yellow perch
- Castle River: Rainbow and cutthroat trout
- North Saskatchewan River: Walleye, northern pike, goldeye

LICENSE REQUIREMENTS
- All anglers 16+ must have a valid Alberta fishing license
- Available online at albertarelm.com or authorized vendors
- Annual licenses and short-term options available
- Some waters require additional special licenses

CATCH LIMITS
- Limits vary by species and body of water
- Always consult current Alberta Guide to Sportfishing Regulations
- Practice catch-and-release on pressured waters
- Keep only what you'll eat

BEST PRACTICES
- Check ice thickness before ice fishing (minimum 4 inches for foot travel)
- Respect private property and obtain permission
- Pack out all garbage and fishing line
- Use barbless hooks when practicing catch-and-release
- Report poaching: 1-800-642-3800

STOCKING PROGRAM
Alberta regularly stocks lakes with trout, walleye, and other species. Check the Alberta Fish Stocking List for recently stocked waters near you.

For current regulations, closures, and detailed information, always refer to the official Alberta Guide to Sportfishing Regulations published by Alberta Environment and Protected Areas.
