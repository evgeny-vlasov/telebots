SOUTHERN ALBERTA FISHING CALENDAR
================================================================================
Month-by-Month Guide for the Calgary Area and Surrounding Region

This calendar covers the prime fishing opportunities throughout the year in
southern Alberta, focusing on the Calgary area, Bow River, and accessible
prairie waters. Seasons and conditions can vary by 1-2 weeks depending on
weather patterns.

================================================================================
JANUARY - MIDWINTER ICE FISHING
================================================================================

Species Active: Pike, Walleye (slow), Rainbow Trout (stocked ponds)

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Crawling Valley Reservoir, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Peak ice thickness, safe travel on most lakes
Methods: Tip-ups with dead bait (cisco, smelt), large spoons jigged aggressively
Best Time: Midday 10am-3pm when sun warms shallows
Locations: Weed edges in 8-15 feet, especially Ghost Lake
Tips: Pike are sluggish; slow presentation near bottom, use large 4-6" baits

WALLEYE - Chain Lakes, Travers Reservoir, McGregor Lake
Conditions: Fish deep structure
Methods: Jigging spoons, jigging Rapalas, blade baits on bottom
Best Time: Low light - dawn/dusk, or night fishing under lights
Locations: Rocky points, old creek channels in 20-35 feet
Tips: Very slow presentation required, scent helps, be patient

STOCKED RAINBOW TROUT - Urban Ponds (if not frozen solid)
Conditions: Some ponds remain partially open near aerators
Methods: Small jigs under ice, PowerBait on bottom
Best Time: Midday
Tips: Fish near aerators or inflow areas, trout less active in cold

General January Tips:
• Ice thickness generally 18-24"+ by mid-January
• Check conditions before heading out - early season ice or late season always risky
• Dress in layers, watch for frostbite
• Fishing slows; patience and persistence key

================================================================================
FEBRUARY - LATE ICE BITE BEGINS
================================================================================

Species Active: Pike (improving), Walleye (improving), Rainbow Trout (stocked ponds)

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Late ice bite starting as days lengthen
Methods: Tip-ups, jigging spoons, quick-strike rigs
Best Time: All day as fish become more active, still best midday
Locations: Moving from deep winter haunts toward shallower bays
Tips: Pike start feeding more aggressively toward month end, increase retrieve speed

WALLEYE - Chain Lakes, Travers Reservoir
Conditions: Walleye begin to stage near spawning areas
Methods: Jigging with minnows, blade baits, glow lures at night
Best Time: Evening bite improves significantly
Locations: Points and flats near tributaries in 15-25 feet
Tips: Pre-spawn walleye feeding heavily, best ice walleye fishing of season

General February Tips:
• Late ice can offer best fishing of ice season
• Longer days improve bite windows
• Start thinking about spring stocking - ponds receive fish soon
• Ice conditions vary - always check, especially late month as melt begins

================================================================================
MARCH - SPRING TRANSITION (ICE-OUT BEGINS)
================================================================================

Species Active: Rainbow Trout (stocked ponds - ice-out), Mountain Whitefish (Bow River)

RAINBOW TROUT - Recently Stocked Ponds (as ice melts)
Conditions: Ice-out on urban ponds, some lakes still frozen
Methods: Spin casting with spoons, spinners (Panther Martins, Mepps), PowerBait
Best Time: Afternoon as water warms slightly
Locations: Urban ponds (Pine Creek, Sikome, Murray Lake), foothills ponds
Tips: Trout very active post ice-out, hungry and aggressive, easy fishing

MOUNTAIN WHITEFISH - Bow River
Conditions: Open water, cold temps, beginning of excellent whitefish season
Methods: Float fishing with roe or worms, nymphing under indicator, small jigs
Best Time: Midday 11am-3pm
Locations: Bow River pools and runs in Calgary and upstream
Tips: Whitefish abundant and willing, great for beginners, good eating

BOW RIVER - General
Conditions: Cold water, pre-runoff clarity
Status: Often catch-and-release only for trout - check regs
Methods: Nymphing, streamer fishing for trout
Tips: Trout fishing can be good but check seasonal closures on some sections

General March Tips:
• Ice leaves prairie lakes by end of month (weather dependent)
• Early stocking begins - check stocking schedule
• Bow River flows low and clear, excellent visibility
• Prepare gear for upcoming season

================================================================================
APRIL - SPRING FISHING HEATS UP
================================================================================

Species Active: Rainbow Trout (stocked ponds), Pike, Walleye (spawning),
Mountain Whitefish, Goldeye (returning)

RAINBOW TROUT - Stocked Ponds Throughout Region
Conditions: Excellent - first major stocking wave
Methods: All methods work - spinners, spoons, flies, PowerBait, float fishing
Best Time: Morning and evening, all day on overcast days
Locations: Newly stocked ponds (check stocking reports), foothills lakes
Tips: Fish are fresh, hungry, and eager - best easy trout fishing of year
Note: Many ponds stocked BEFORE MAY 23 and during JUNE 1-15 period

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Crawling Valley
Conditions: Pre-spawn and spawning, pike in shallows
Methods: Spoons, spinners, jerkbaits in shallow bays
Best Time: Morning, all day when overcast
Locations: Shallow weedy bays 2-6 feet deep
Tips: Aggressive feeding pre-spawn, pike readily hit large lures

WALLEYE - Chain Lakes, McGregor Lake, Travers Reservoir
Conditions: Spawning season - check for closures
Methods: Jigs tipped with minnows (where legal), crankbaits
Best Time: Evening and night
Locations: Shallow rocky areas, tributary mouths
Tips: Handle spawning walleye carefully, many waters have spring closures

MOUNTAIN WHITEFISH - Bow River
Conditions: Excellent - peak season
Methods: Float fishing with roe/worms, small spinners, flies
Best Time: Midday
Locations: All Bow River pools and runs
Tips: Easy fishing, great for kids and beginners

General April Tips:
• Check stocking schedules - ponds receive rainbow trout
• Watch for walleye/pike closures during spawning
• Weather variable - be prepared for anything
• Bow runoff typically late month or early May

================================================================================
MAY - PEAK STOCKING SEASON & SPRING FISHING
================================================================================

Species Active: Rainbow Trout (PEAK), Goldeye (excellent), Pike, Walleye (post-spawn)

RAINBOW TROUT - Stocked Ponds (PRIME TIME)
Conditions: Main stocking push BEFORE MAY 23 and JUNE 1-15
Methods: All methods - spinners, spoons, float fishing with bait, flies
Best Time: Morning 6-10am, evening 6-9pm, all day early season
Locations: Every stocked pond receives fish - check current stocking reports
Tips: Fresh stocker trout fishing at its peak, fish hungry and aggressive
Key: Many ponds stocked in 1-2 waves so check dates for your favorite location

GOLDEYE - Bow River below Carseland Weir
Conditions: Excellent - goldeye run begins
Methods: Float fishing with worms or roe, small spinners, flies (dry flies work!)
Best Time: Morning and evening, focus on dawn
Locations: Carseland Weir downstream to Bassano, eddies and pools
Tips: Goldeye are incredible fighters on light tackle, underrated species
Note: Watch for regulations - some areas catch-and-release, check limits

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Post-spawn recovery, moving to summer patterns
Methods: Spoons, spinnerbaits, topwater lures (late May)
Best Time: Morning and evening
Locations: Transition from shallows to weed edges in 8-12 feet
Tips: Pike fishing improves through May as fish recover and feed heavily

WALLEYE - Prairie Reservoirs
Conditions: Post-spawn feeding, fish scattered
Methods: Jigs and soft plastics, crankbaits, live bait rigs
Best Time: Evening prime time, night fishing productive
Locations: Rocky points, flats adjacent to spawning areas
Tips: Post-spawn walleye hungry, aggressive - excellent fishing

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary and Upstream
Conditions: Pre-runoff or early runoff - check flows
Methods: Nymphing, dry flies (emerging BWOs, midges), streamers
Best Time: Midday
Tips: If runoff hasn't started, excellent fishing; once runoff hits, fishing gets tough

General May Tips:
• Peak stocking season - BEFORE MAY 23 for many ponds
• Goldeye fishing begins - don't overlook this species
• Bow River runoff typically mid-May to mid-June
• Prairie lakes warming - all species active
• Licenses renew April 1 - ensure yours is current

================================================================================
JUNE - RUNOFF & EARLY SUMMER
================================================================================

Species Active: Rainbow Trout (stocked ponds), Goldeye (peak), Pike, Walleye

RAINBOW TROUT - Stocked Ponds
Conditions: Second stocking wave JUNE 1-15 for many waters
Methods: All methods effective, float fishing with bait excellent
Best Time: Early morning and evening as water warms
Locations: Check for June stockings - many ponds get second round
Tips: June stockers supplement May fish, providing fresh opportunities
Water Temps: Trout stressed above 20°C - fish mornings or high elevation

GOLDEYE - Bow River below Carseland (PEAK SEASON)
Conditions: Runoff makes Bow main stem tough, but goldeye bite stays strong
Methods: Float fishing with worms, roe, small spinners in slower water
Best Time: Early morning at dawn is magic hour
Locations: Carseland to Bassano, eddies and slower pools below weir
Tips: Goldeye fishing peaks in June, incredible sport, fight like crazy
Note: While Bow trout fishing suffers in runoff, goldeye fishing thrives

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Excellent - summer pattern establishing
Methods: Spinnerbaits, spoons, topwater (especially late June), jerkbaits
Best Time: Early morning, evening, overcast days all day
Locations: Weed edges, shallow bays, near structure in 6-12 feet
Tips: Pike aggressive and feeding heavily, topwater fishing begins

WALLEYE - Chain Lakes, McGregor, Travers, Newell
Conditions: Good - summer pattern developing
Methods: Jigs and plastics, bottom bouncers with worms, crankbaits
Best Time: Evening and night are best, dawn also good
Locations: Rocky points, drop-offs in 15-25 feet during day, shallower at night
Tips: Walleye move to deeper structure as water warms, focus on structure

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary and Upstream
Conditions: Runoff peak - usually difficult fishing
Methods: Wait for flows to drop, or fish small tributaries
Best Time: Not prime time - wait for runoff to end
Tips: High turbid water makes Bow trout fishing tough until runoff clears (usually late June)

General June Tips:
• Second stocking wave JUNE 1-15 - check schedules
• Bow River runoff makes trout fishing difficult but goldeye fishing excellent
• Prairie lakes excellent for pike and walleye
• Water temps rising - fish early mornings and evenings
• Camping and fishing combos popular - lakes accessible

================================================================================
JULY - SUMMER PATTERNS & HOT WEATHER
================================================================================

Species Active: Rainbow Trout (high elevation), Goldeye, Pike, Walleye,
Mountain Whitefish, Bow River Trout (post-runoff)

RAINBOW TROUT - High Elevation Lakes and Cool Ponds
Conditions: Lowland ponds too warm - focus on elevation or spring-fed waters
Methods: Float fishing with bait, spinners, flies
Best Time: Early morning before heat, late evening
Locations: Mountain lakes, foothills reservoirs, spring-fed ponds
Tips: Trout need cool water - focus on high elevation or deep lakes
Heat Stress: Below 15°C ideal, okay to 20°C, stressed above 20°C - land and release quickly

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary Upstream (POST-RUNOFF)
Conditions: Excellent - runoff cleared, water cooling, best summer trout fishing
Methods: Dry flies (hoppers, caddis, mayflies), nymphing, streamers
Best Time: Morning and evening prime, midday if overcast
Locations: Downtown Calgary through Banff - all sections productive
Tips: Post-runoff is PRIME time for Bow River trout - world-class fishery
Special: Hopper fishing begins - trout smash terrestrials along banks

GOLDEYE - Bow River below Carseland
Conditions: Still good but peak passing
Methods: Float fishing with worms, flies, small spinners
Best Time: Early morning
Locations: Carseland to Bassano
Tips: Goldeye bite continues but slows as summer progresses

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Good early morning and evening
Methods: Topwater lures (buzzbaits, poppers), spinnerbaits, spoons
Best Time: Dawn and dusk critical in heat, or overcast days
Locations: Weed edges, near structure, shaded areas
Tips: Hot water makes midday fishing slow - focus on low light periods

WALLEYE - Prairie Reservoirs
Conditions: Deep summer pattern - focus on structure
Methods: Jigs and plastics, crankbaits, live bait on bottom
Best Time: Night fishing prime, dawn/dusk good, midday deep water only
Locations: Deep rocky structure 20-35 feet during day, shallow points at night
Tips: Walleye go deep or move to shallows only at night, adjust accordingly

MOUNTAIN WHITEFISH - Bow River
Conditions: Good in deeper pools
Methods: Float fishing with worms/roe, nymphing
Best Time: Midday
Tips: Reliable option when trout are finicky, excellent table fare

General July Tips:
• Bow River trout fishing excellent post-runoff (usually by July)
• Hot weather pushes fish to early morning/late evening activity
• Prairie lakes best at dawn/dusk
• Stay hydrated, use sun protection
• Water levels dropping on reservoirs - watch for low water hazards

================================================================================
AUGUST - LATE SUMMER FISHING
================================================================================

Species Active: Bow River Trout, Pike, Walleye, Rainbow Trout (high elevation)

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary Upstream (EXCELLENT)
Conditions: Prime time continues - stable flows, good hatches
Methods: Hopper-dropper rigs, dry flies, streamers, nymphing
Best Time: Morning and evening best, full day fishing possible
Locations: All Bow River sections from Calgary to Banff
Tips: August can be peak Bow River season - hoppers, ants, beetles all work
Note: Watch water temps - if above 18°C, fish quickly and gently

RAINBOW TROUT - Mountain Lakes
Conditions: Mountain lakes at prime temperatures
Methods: All methods, float fishing and flies particularly good
Best Time: All day in mountains, cooler temps extend feeding
Locations: Kananaskis lakes, Banff area lakes, high elevation waters
Tips: Mountain lake trout fishing peaks in August - perfect temperatures

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Early morning/evening patterns continue
Methods: Topwater early and late, deep diving crankbaits midday
Best Time: First light and last light best
Locations: Deeper weed edges during day, shallows morning/evening
Tips: Some of year's best topwater action at dawn

WALLEYE - Prairie Reservoirs
Conditions: Deep summer patterns, night fishing best
Methods: Jigs, crankbaits, bottom bouncers, live bait
Best Time: Night fishing excellent, dusk/dawn good
Locations: Rocky structure, transition zones
Tips: Focus on night bite for best action

General August Tips:
• Bow River remains excellent for trout
• Mountain lakes hit prime temperature window
• Prairie fishing best early/late or night
• Watch for algae blooms in some lakes
• Great month for camping/fishing trips

================================================================================
SEPTEMBER - FALL TRANSITION & FANTASTIC FISHING
================================================================================

Species Active: ALL SPECIES - Pike (peak), Walleye, Rainbow Trout (fall stocking),
Bow River Trout (excellent), Mountain Whitefish

RAINBOW TROUT - Stocked Ponds (Fall Stocking Wave)
Conditions: Fall stockings begin (Sept 15-30 typical)
Methods: All methods work, fish aggressive pre-winter
Best Time: All day as temps cool
Locations: Same ponds as spring, check stocking reports
Tips: Fall stockers plus spring survivors make for excellent fishing

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary Upstream (PEAK SEASON)
Conditions: Some of year's best Bow fishing - cool temps, hungry trout
Methods: Streamers (especially), nymphs, dry flies (BWOs, October caddis)
Best Time: All day productive
Locations: All Bow River sections
Tips: Big browns and rainbows feed heavily pre-winter, streamer fishing shines
Note: October Caddis hatch can be phenomenal

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes (PRIME TIME)
Conditions: Fall turnover brings BEST pike fishing of year
Methods: Large spoons, jerkbaits, crankbaits, topwater (early month)
Best Time: All day as temperatures drop
Locations: Shallow and mid-depth areas, actively hunting
Tips: Aggressive pre-winter feeding, pike hit hard and fast, trophy opportunities

WALLEYE - Prairie Reservoirs (EXCELLENT)
Conditions: Fall feeding binge begins
Methods: Jigs and plastics, crankbaits, live bait rigs
Best Time: Extended bite windows - dusk through dawn
Locations: Points, flats, scattered throughout water column
Tips: Walleye feed aggressively to prepare for winter, excellent fishing

MOUNTAIN WHITEFISH - Bow River
Conditions: Excellent and improving
Methods: Float fishing with roe/worms, nymphing, small spinners
Best Time: Midday best
Locations: All Bow River pools
Tips: Whitefish congregate and feed heavily, great action

General September Tips:
• One of best overall fishing months of year
• All species active as temps cool
• Fall stocking wave arrives (Sept 15-30 typical)
• Pike fishing hits annual peak
• Bow River trout fishing excellent
• Comfortable temperatures for fishing all day

================================================================================
OCTOBER - PEAK FALL FISHING
================================================================================

Species Active: Rainbow Trout, Bow River Trout (peak), Pike (peak continues),
Walleye, Mountain Whitefish (peak)

RAINBOW TROUT - Stocked Ponds and Lakes
Conditions: Excellent - cool water, aggressive fish
Methods: All methods, spoons and spinners particularly effective
Best Time: Midday as mornings/evenings get cold
Locations: All stocked waters with fall fish
Tips: Fall trout fishing excellent, fish heavy and healthy

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary Upstream (PEAK)
Conditions: Arguably best month for Bow trout - spawning activity, feeding
Methods: Streamers (browns spawning), nymphs, October Caddis dry flies
Best Time: All day
Locations: All sections, focus on riffles and runs for spawning activity
Tips: Trophy brown trout opportunities peak in October, streamer fishing excellent
Ethics: Respect spawning fish - don't target fish on redds (spawning beds)

MOUNTAIN WHITEFISH - Bow River (PEAK SEASON)
Conditions: Best whitefish fishing of year
Methods: Float fishing with worms or roe, small jigs, nymphing
Best Time: Midday 10am-3pm
Locations: Bow River pools throughout Calgary and upstream
Tips: Whitefish schools concentrated and aggressive, excellent table fare

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes (CONTINUES)
Conditions: Excellent - fall bite continues
Methods: Jerkbaits, large spoons, crankbaits, spinners
Best Time: All day, especially when sunny
Locations: Mid-depths 8-15 feet, actively hunting
Tips: Pike remain aggressive all month, trophy fishing good

WALLEYE - Prairie Reservoirs
Conditions: Excellent fall bite continues
Methods: Jigs with plastics or live bait, crankbaits
Best Time: Extended windows, all evening good
Locations: Points and flats in 12-25 feet
Tips: Walleye continue aggressive fall feeding

General October Tips:
• Peak month for Bow River trout (browns spawning)
• Mountain whitefish at their best
• Pike and walleye continue strong
• Weather variable - dress in layers
• Some of year's best fishing across all species
• Shorter days - plan accordingly

================================================================================
NOVEMBER - LATE FALL & ICE-ON
================================================================================

Species Active: Bow River Trout (good), Mountain Whitefish (excellent),
Rainbow Trout (stocked ponds), Early ice fishing begins

MOUNTAIN WHITEFISH - Bow River (EXCELLENT)
Conditions: Peak season continues
Methods: Float fishing with roe or worms, nymphing
Best Time: Midday during warmest hours
Locations: Deep pools on Bow River
Tips: Cold-water species, reliable when trout slow down, concentrating in pools

BOW RIVER TROUT - Calgary Upstream
Conditions: Good on warmer days
Methods: Nymphing primarily, streamers, small dries on warm afternoons
Best Time: Midday during temperature peaks
Locations: Deep runs and pools, slower water
Tips: Trout slow but still active on warmer days, small flies and presentations

RAINBOW TROUT - Stocked Ponds (before freeze-up)
Conditions: Slowing as ice forms, some ponds already frozen
Methods: Small spinners, spoons, PowerBait on bottom
Best Time: Midday
Locations: Ponds still ice-free
Tips: Fishing slows significantly, but some opportunities remain

EARLY ICE FISHING - Prairie Lakes (late November)
Species: Pike, Walleye
Conditions: First ice forming on prairie lakes - ALWAYS check ice safety first
Methods: Tip-ups, jigging spoons
Best Time: All day
Locations: Ghost Lake, Crawling Valley (if safe ice)
Tips: Early ice can be dangerous - 4" clear ice minimum, avoid pressure cracks
Note: Some of best ice fishing of year but SAFETY FIRST always

General November Tips:
• Bow River remains open - whitefish and trout available
• Prairie lakes begin freezing - monitor ice conditions
• Early ice fishing possible late month if conditions allow
• Cold weather gear essential
• Shorter days - limited fishing windows
• Prepare gear for ice fishing season

================================================================================
DECEMBER - EARLY ICE & WINTER PATTERNS
================================================================================

Species Active: Pike (ice fishing), Walleye (ice fishing), Rainbow Trout (ice fishing)

ICE FISHING - Prairie Lakes and Stocked Ponds
Conditions: Ice fishing season underway - always check ice thickness
Methods: Tip-ups with dead bait (pike), jigging spoons and jigs (walleye, trout)
Best Time: Midday when sun provides warmth
Species: Pike, walleye, rainbow trout depending on water
Tips: Early season ice best fishing but requires constant safety vigilance

NORTHERN PIKE - Ghost Lake, Prairie Lakes
Conditions: Good early ice fishing
Methods: Tip-ups with large baits, jigging with large spoons
Best Time: Midday
Locations: Weed edges in 8-12 feet
Tips: Pike active early ice, will slow as winter progresses

WALLEYE - Chain Lakes, Travers, McGregor
Conditions: Good if ice is safe
Methods: Jigging with minnows, blade baits, jigs
Best Time: Dawn, dusk, and night under lights
Locations: Points and structure in 15-30 feet
Tips: Walleye bite good early ice, slow presentation key

BOW RIVER - Open Water (limited)
Conditions: Open in some sections, cold water
Methods: Nymphing for whitefish and trout
Best Time: Midday only during warm spells
Tips: Limited activity, whitefish more reliable than trout

General December Tips:
• Ice fishing season begins - safety first always
• Check ice conditions constantly - 4" minimum clear ice
• Bow River open but fishing slow
• Short days limit fishing time
• Early ice provides best opportunities before full winter sets in
• Prepare for cold - proper clothing essential

YEAR-ROUND TIPS FOR SOUTHERN ALBERTA
================================================================================

Stocking Schedule Key Dates:
• Early Spring: Before May 23rd (main stocking push)
• Late Spring: June 1-15 (second wave many ponds)
• Fall: September 15-30 (fall stocking)
• Check current schedules at alberta.ca/fish-stocking

Best Overall Months:
• Trout (stocked): May, June, September, October
• Trout (Bow River): July, August, September, October
• Pike: September, October, May, early ice December
• Walleye: May, September, October, February (ice)
• Whitefish: April, September, October, November
• Goldeye: May, June, July

Float Fishing Applications:
Float fishing (slip bobber or fixed) is effective year-round:
• Rainbow trout in stocked ponds: bait under float at 3-6 feet
• Goldeye in Bow River: worms/roe under float in current
• Mountain whitefish: roe or worms under float in pools
• Pike (ice fishing): tip-ups are essentially float fishing through ice
• Walleye: live bait under slip bobber near structure

Equipment Tips:
• Light tackle (4-6 lb test) for trout and whitefish
• Medium tackle (8-12 lb) for pike and walleye
• Ultra-light spinning setups ideal for Bow River
• Quality polarized sunglasses essential for sight fishing
• Waders or warm boots depending on season

Ethics and Conservation:
• Follow all regulations and closures
• Practice catch and release where required
• Handle fish quickly and gently in warm water
• Use barbless hooks for easier releases
• Keep only what you'll eat, release the rest
• Report violations: 1-800-642-3800
• Kill all Prussian carp - mandatory by law

Southern Alberta offers incredible diversity in fishing opportunities throughout
the year. Whether you're targeting trophy Bow River trout, aggressive fall pike,
or enjoying easy stocked trout fishing with family, there's always something biting.

Plan trips around the seasonal patterns above, watch stocking schedules, and
you'll enjoy some of Canada's best fishing right in southern Alberta.

Tight lines!
